Athena was a scheduling system for drivers who delivered surgical trays on-demand to hospitals. Most hospitals don’t keep every specific kind of surgical tray on-site, and hire outside companies to deliver them when needed.

Organizing a bunch of drivers at a company like this isn’t an easy task. Athena was meant to make that job as simple as possible. Drivers would log in and be able to see how many trays they were scheduled to deliver that day, to where, and when they would need to pick them up. It also featured a robust user account system.

I wrote Athena early on in my programming career, and it is not representative of my current coding style. It was written entirely in PHP, and the only external library used was jQuery.

Unfortunately, the project was canceled after a few months of work due to funding problems.

You can see the project’s code at my GitHub.
